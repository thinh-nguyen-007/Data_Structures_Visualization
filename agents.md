## GRAPH PRINCIPLES
- Graph: A collection of vertices (nodes) and edges connecting them.
- Edge: A connection between two vertices.
- Weighted graph: Edges have associated weights (e.g., distance, cost).
- Unweighted graph: Edges have no weights.
- Directed graph: Edges have a direction (A → B is different from B → A).
- Undirected graph: Edges have no direction (A — B is the same as B — A).
- Tree: An acyclic connected graph (no cycles).
- Forest: A collection of trees (acyclic, may not be connected).
- Cycle: A path that starts and ends at the same vertex.
- Complete graph: Every vertex is connected to every other vertex.
- Sparse graph: Few edges compared to the maximum possible.
- Dense graph: Many edges, close to the maximum possible.
- Connected component: A subgraph where any two vertices are connected by paths.

## TSP PRINCIPLES
- TSP: Find the shortest possible route that visits each city exactly once and returns to the starting city.
- Hamiltonian cycle: A cycle that visits every vertex exactly once.
- Optimal solution: The shortest possible TSP tour.
- Suboptimal solution: A TSP tour that is not the shortest possible.
- Heuristic: A technique that finds a good-enough solution quickly.
